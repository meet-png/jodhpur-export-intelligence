# Field notes — Basni & Boranada exporter conversations

> Use this template **during** the field visit. Fill one section per conversation.
> After the visit, rename this file to `field_notes.md` and commit it.
> Anonymise where the exporter asks; otherwise full names + designations are fine.

---

## How to run the conversation (5-min framework)

You are not a journalist. You are not selling anything. You are a young person from Jodhpur trying to understand the export business better. People will talk to you.

**Open with:** "Namaskar. Mera naam Meet hai, main Jodhpur se hi hoon. Computer engineering kar raha hoon and ek project bana raha hoon export industry ke liye. Aapse 10 minute baat karni thi — aapka data nahi maangoonga, sirf yeh samajhna hai ki business chalane mein sabse mushkil cheez kya hoti hai."

**The single core question:** "Aapke business mein ek aisi cheez kya hai jo paise kharch karwati hai aur jise aap aur ache se samajh paate to fayda hota?"

**Then shut up and listen.** Take notes. Don't interrupt. Ask follow-ups only:
- "Yeh kab tak hota hai?" (How long has this been a problem?)
- "Aap iske bare mein abhi kya karte ho?" (How do you currently handle it?)
- "Agar aapke paas yeh information hoti, kya farak padta?" (If you had this information, what would change?)

**Close with:** "Bahut shukriya. Project ban jaye to dikhane aaunga." (Thank you. Once the project is built, I'll come show you.)

You don't need to follow the script word-for-word. The point is: come in respectful, ask one question, listen.

## Why this document matters for your interviews

When an interviewer asks "what was the most surprising thing you learned in this project?", you don't quote a Pandas DataFrame. You quote a 55-year-old owner in Basni who said *"Hum Christmas ke time hamesha 30% short rehte hain. Pata to hai problem hai, par kabhi number nikaala nahi."*

That moment, in that interview, is when you separate from every other portfolio project on the table.
